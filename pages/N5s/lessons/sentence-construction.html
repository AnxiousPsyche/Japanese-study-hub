Basic Sentence Pattern

PATTERN 1

A は B です

Basic Sentence Pattern

A

↓

Subject

Person / Thing
we are talking about

は

↓

Topic Marker

Indicates the topic.
Similar to "is / am / are" but not exactly.

B

↓

Predicate

Information
about the subject.

です

↓

Polite Ending

Makes the sentence
polite.

EXAMPLES

私はリアです。

Watashi wa Ria desu.

I am Ria.

これは本です。

Kore wa hon desu.

This is a book.

田中さんは先生です。

Tanaka-san wa sensei desu.

Mr. Tanaka is a teacher.

PATTERN 2

S T P O V

Japanese Sentence Order

S

↓

Subject

Who?
Person or thing.

T

↓

Time

When?
Today, tomorrow, yesterday...

P

↓

Place

Where?
School, home, Japan...

O

↓

Object

What?
The thing receiving the action.

V

↓

Verb

Action
Always comes last.

EXAMPLE

私は

Watashi wa

I

今日

Kyou

today

学校で

Gakkou de

at school

日本語を

Nihongo o

Japanese

勉強します。

Benkyou shimasu.

study.

💡 Remember

  • The verb almost always comes at the end of the sentence.
  • Time and place can move depending on what you want to emphasize.
  • Particles (は・を・で・に・へ・と) show the role of each word.
